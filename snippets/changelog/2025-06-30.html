Introducing the Factual Accuracy Metric

We've added a new Factual Accuracy metric to our evaluation toolkit! This metric measures whether the information in your model's output is factually correct when compared to a trusted reference answer. It's particularly valuable for RAG and question answering systems where accuracy is paramount.

The metric uses an LLM-as-a-judge approach to compare key facts between your model's output and the expected answer, helping you catch hallucinations and ensure your AI provides reliable information to users. Read the full documentation here.


Enhanced Red Teaming with New Attack Strategies

Our red teaming capabilities just got more sophisticated! We've added two powerful new attack strategies:

  • Biblical Strategy: Transforms adversarial prompts into biblical/ancient scripture style to disguise malicious intent. Learn more
  • Math Prompt Strategy: Encodes harmful requests into formal mathematical notation to obscure intent from standard text analysis. See all strategies

These strategies join our existing arsenal to help you test your AI's defenses against increasingly creative attack vectors that real-world adversaries might use. Explore all red teaming strategies.


Smarter Red Teaming Test Generation

We've significantly improved how red teaming tests are generated. Our system now takes even more factors into account when creating adversarial test cases:

  • Product-Aware Generation: Tests are now more precisely tailored to your specific product's strengths and boundaries.
  • Context-Sensitive Attacks: Better understanding of your product's intended use cases to craft more relevant scenarios.
  • Enhanced Threat Modeling: Broader consideration of factors when determining the most effective attack vectors.

This means your red teaming tests will be more effective at uncovering real vulnerabilities specific to your product. Read more about red teaming tests.


Better Metric Source Visibility and Management

Understanding where your metrics come from is now easier than ever! We've enhanced the platform to provide clearer visibility into metric sources:

  • Source Classification: All metrics are clearly labeled with their source and origin.
  • Enhanced Filtering: Filter metrics by their source to find the right evaluation criteria.
  • Improved Descriptions: More detailed information about origins and implementation with documentation links.

The three main metric sources you'll see are:

  • Galtea: Custom metrics designed specifically for your needs, like our new Factual Accuracy metric
  • G-Eval: Framework-based metrics that use evaluation criteria or steps for assessment
  • Established Frameworks: Metrics adapted from proven evaluation libraries and methodologies
